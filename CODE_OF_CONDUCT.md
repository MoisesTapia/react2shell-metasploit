# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our
community include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the
  overall community
* Using this tool responsibly and ethically for authorized security research only

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or
  advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email
  address, without their explicit permission
* Discussing or sharing techniques for unauthorized system access
* Sharing malicious code or exploits intended for harmful purposes
* Encouraging or assisting in illegal activities
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Security Research Ethics

As this project is focused on security research, we have additional standards:

### Responsible Use
* Only use this tool against systems you own or have explicit written permission to test
* Follow responsible disclosure practices when discovering vulnerabilities
* Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
* Respect the privacy and security of others

### Prohibited Activities
* Using this tool for unauthorized access to systems
* Sharing techniques or information that could facilitate illegal activities
* Discussing specific vulnerabilities in production systems without proper disclosure
* Providing assistance for malicious purposes

### Educational Focus
* Share knowledge constructively to improve security
* Help others learn ethical security research practices
* Promote responsible vulnerability disclosure
* Support the security research community

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when
an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.
Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail address,
posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed
representative at an online or offline event.

This Code of Conduct also applies to the use of this security tool in any context,
as the reputation of the project and community is affected by how the tool is used.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at
[security@yourproject.com].

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining
the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the
behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series
of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels
like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or
permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or sharing of malicious content.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or
private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction
with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.
Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.
This also includes using the tool for malicious purposes or assisting others
in illegal activities.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within
the community.

## Security-Specific Enforcement

For violations related to the misuse of this security tool:

### 1. Educational Response

**Impact**: Minor misunderstanding about ethical use or responsible disclosure.

**Response**: Educational guidance about proper security research practices,
responsible disclosure, and legal considerations.

### 2. Formal Warning

**Impact**: Inappropriate discussion of vulnerabilities or techniques that could
facilitate unauthorized access.

**Response**: Formal warning about responsible disclosure practices and community
standards. Temporary restriction from security-related discussions.

### 3. Community Suspension

**Impact**: Sharing malicious code, discussing illegal activities, or encouraging
unauthorized system access.

**Response**: Temporary suspension from the community with required education
about security ethics before reinstatement consideration.

### 4. Permanent Exclusion

**Impact**: Using the tool for malicious purposes, assisting in illegal activities,
or repeatedly violating security ethics guidelines.

**Response**: Permanent exclusion from the community and reporting to appropriate
authorities if illegal activities are involved.

## Reporting Guidelines

### General Issues
Report general code of conduct violations to: [conduct@yourproject.com]

### Security Ethics Issues
Report security-related violations to: [security@yourproject.com]

### What to Include
* Description of the incident
* Date and time of occurrence
* Individuals involved (if known)
* Any relevant evidence (screenshots, logs, etc.)
* Impact on the community or security

### Confidentiality
All reports will be handled confidentially. We will not share reporter information
without explicit consent, except as required by law.

## Legal Considerations

### Disclaimer
This Code of Conduct does not constitute legal advice. Users are responsible for
understanding and complying with all applicable laws in their jurisdiction.

### Cooperation with Authorities
We will cooperate with law enforcement when illegal activities are reported or
discovered, while protecting the privacy of community members to the extent
legally possible.

### International Considerations
Security research laws vary by country. Community members are responsible for
understanding the legal implications of security research in their jurisdiction
and any jurisdictions where they conduct research.
